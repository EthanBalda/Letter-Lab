The Letters Rules:

a; Apathetic
Does nothing. Other letters can interact with it.

b; Basic : aba -> aab OR baa
Swaps places with a cardinally adjacent letter.

c; Complex : aacaa -> caaaa OR aaaac
Swaps places with a letter cardinally adjacent letter one slot away from it. One more space than b.

d; Delete : adb -> ad_
Deletes whatever letter tries to interact with it. Has some weird interactions.

e; Eat : abcec -> ab_cc
Eats any adjacent letter, deleting that letter and becoming it.

f; Force : abfcc -> bfcca OR cabfc
Moves the entire string or grid cardinally one space. If a letter would be knocked off the string, it wraps back to the start.

g; Grab : abcagbb -> bcaagbb OR abacgbb OR acabgbb
Grabs a cardinally adjacent letter, pulls it adjacent to ‘g’, then moves all other letters back to fill space.

h; Hold (This may need to be removed with grids) : ahb -> ahb_
Holds a selected letter over its head, effectively removing it from the string or grid. Can swap out whatever is held with another letter. If it grabs a ‘d’, the ‘h’ and whatever it was holding get deleted, and the ‘d’ moves to the ‘h’ ‘s location. Can only hold and swap any adjacent letter.

i; Immovable : acbi -> acbi
Cannot be interacted with by anything other than ‘n’.

j; Jump : abcjcba -> jbcacba OR abcacbj
Like ‘c’, but with 1 more range.

k; Kick : bkabba -> bkbbaa
The exact opposite of ‘g’. Kicks any cardinally adjacent letter to the edge of the grid or string, then shifts every letter towards it to fill in the gap.

l; Lovely (These should probably be ditched if we commit to grids.) : bacblccbb -> bbaclcbbc
Pulls every cardinal letter into it whenever it is selected. Letters that would move to the selected ‘l’s slot instead go to the edges.

m; Monstrous (These should probably be ditched if we commit to grids.) : bacbmccbb -> acbbmbccb
Opposite of ‘l’, they scare every cardinal letter one slot away. Any letters that would go off the grid, instead end up in the empty adjacent slots.

The Letters Rules, N-Z (LetterLab 2.0)

Adapted from LetterLab_FinalProjectFall2024.pdf for the grid engine and its
one-origin-one-target selection model. Where 2.0 deviates from the original
design, the deviation and its reason are noted inline.

Universal laws (unchanged from a-m, and every new letter obeys them):
- d never moves on its own and destroys any letter that interacts with it.
- i cannot be moved or targeted by anything; sliding letters pass over it.
- Exactly one origin tap and at most one target tap per move.

n; Nullify : with n at the end: aabcn -> the c (adjacent to n) cannot act
Passive aura. Letters cardinally adjacent to an n lose their abilities and
behave exactly like 'a': selectable letters cannot act, d stops deleting
(and can be moved!), and i loses its protection. Two adjacent n's nullify
each other, switching both auras off (no negative stacking). n itself
never acts; other letters can move it around like an 'a'.
[2.0 notes: original name "No" -> "Nullify" (more descriptive, still one
word). The aura is implemented as "the letter effectively becomes 'a'",
which uniformly produces every clarification in the original doc: nullified
d's no longer delete, nullified i's are the only touchable i's, making n
the counter to both.]

o; Onomatopoeia : abaobba -> obaabba OR aboabba OR abaabbo
Swaps places with any vowel on the board (a, e, u, or another o), at any
distance. It cannot reach i - no letter can.

p; Purge : aapd -> a___ (fires on selection, like l and m)
Needs no target: the moment you select it, it destroys itself and all four
cardinal neighbours - the only letter that can destroy a d. Only i
survives the blast.
[2.0 note: the original example showed p deleting an i. 2.0 keeps i
absolute (only n gets through), preserving i's identity and n's niche.]

q; Queen : like chess - any distance along its row, column, or diagonal
Swaps with any letter any distance away in a straight line: the whole row,
the whole column, and both diagonals. Distance doesn't matter and letters
in between don't block the swap. Touching a d still destroys her.
[2.0 note: original q was "swap with anything except i and d". Restricting
her to chess-queen lines earns the name, leaves z a throne to claim, and
keeps the universal d rule intact instead of special-casing it away.]

r; Remove : accdrca (select the adjacent c) -> a__dr_a with r becoming a
Targets an adjacent letter and removes every copy of that letter from the
board, then becomes an 'a' itself. Selecting an adjacent d removes every
d - but consumes the r entirely. It cannot remove i's or blanks.
[2.0 note: original allowed removing i's; 2.0 keeps i absolute.]

s; Spin : bsa -> asb
Swaps the selected adjacent letter with the letter directly opposite it
through the s. The s itself never moves. If the opposite cell holds a d,
the selected letter is destroyed against it.

t; Toggle : btc (select the b) -> atc
Transforms an adjacent letter into the previous letter of the alphabet:
c becomes b, b becomes a, and a wraps around to z. Targeting a d is
suicide, as always; i cannot be targeted.

u; Upgrade : buc (select the b) -> cuc
Transforms an adjacent letter into the next letter of the alphabet:
a becomes b, b becomes c, and z wraps around to a. The exact mirror of t.
[2.0 note: renamed from "Undo" - the game has an undo button, and a letter
by the same name is a usability landmine. "Upgrade" reads instantly.]

v; Virus : abvac (select the b) -> avvac
Swaps with an adjacent letter like b - but the letter it swaps with
becomes a v. Swapping into a d destroys the v, and the d catches the
virus: it becomes a v itself. The only thing that ever transforms a d.
i is immune, as ever.

w; Waltz : swaps with any diagonal neighbour
Glides diagonally: swaps with any of its four diagonal neighbours. The
only letter that moves diagonally by itself. A d on the diagonal still
destroys it.
[2.0 note: original w only stepped up-left, wrapping at edges. One fixed
direction is arbitrary on a grid and frustrating to route; "the diagonal
mover" is cleaner, still unique, and keeps the dance.]

x; Xerox : caxb (select the b) -> cabb
Becomes an exact copy of an adjacent letter; the original stays where it
is. Copying an h copies what it holds. It can even photocopy a d - the
only way to create one - but cannot copy an i or a blank.
[2.0 note: original x transformed letter A into letter B, needing two
targets. Copying the target onto itself keeps the photocopy fantasy in a
single tap and stays just as combo-rich.]

y; Yeet : yaab (select the a to its right) -> aaby
Hurls itself: picks a cardinal direction and flies to the far edge of the
board in that direction; every letter it sails past shifts one cell back
to fill the gap. Kick, applied to yourself. Landing on a d is fatal; i's
are sailed over.
[2.0 note: original y jumped by counting vowels in the direction of
travel. Vowel-counting is unreadable at a glance and drifts as the board
changes; self-kick keeps the yeet and pairs beautifully with k.]

z; Zap : swaps with any letter, anywhere
The final letter: swaps with any letter or blank cell anywhere on the
board. No range limit, no line requirement. Only i is beyond its reach,
and a d still ends it.
[2.0 note: original z swapped any two letters (two targets). Letting z
itself teleport-swap keeps the omnipotence in one tap; q keeps the lines,
z gets the whole board.]

Interaction quick-reference for the new letters:
- Ways to deal with a d: e eats it, p bombs it, r purges all of them,
  v infects it, n nullifies it (it can then be moved like an 'a'),
  g/k/l/m sling it destructively, x can create more of them.
- Ways to deal with an i: n nullifies it; nothing else touches it.
- t/u/x/v change what letters exist; r/p/d/e change how many.
- Suppressed letters (next to an active n) count as 'a' for every rule:
  they cannot act, cannot delete, cannot protect themselves.
- Held letters (inside an h) have no aura and no abilities; an h holding
  something is still just 'h' for sorting purposes.
